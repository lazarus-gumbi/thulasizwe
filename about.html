About Thulasizwe

Who is He?

He is currently the anchor of the weekend edition of the news programme ‘Today’ on eNCA, and also hosts the ‘eNCA Corruption Scorecard’, a show geared towards exacting accountability for matters of governance in the public and private sector. He is known for his uncompromising interviewing style, in-depth research into topics he covers, as well as his no-holds-barred opinionated inputs



Career

Over the course of his career, Simelane has been at the forefront of the coverage of some of the major turning points of this country, continent and region's recent history. In 2019, Simelane had a brief stint with the new news channel, Newzroom Afrika, where he headed up the political news desk. He also anchored the afternoon news programme - Newsfeed.



Experience

His formative years in journalism were spent at the SABC, where he held a number of roles including those of senior political journalist for both radio and TV. This placed him in the front row of covering developments such as Julius Malema's rise through the ANC Youth League, the ANC's centenary celebrations in 2012, policy and elective conferences, the politics of the opposition and ANC tripartite alliance partners and labour. Leadership

In 2007, Simelane was entrusted with establishing and running the SABC's bureau in Harare, Zimbabwe. This would thrust him in the middle of, arguably the most challenging story of his career and see him bear witness to such events as that country's 2008 elections, political violence, cholera outbreak and negotiations towards, and formation of, a government of national unity. As a result, Simelane has returned multiple times to Zimbabwe in the latter years of his career and reported extensively on the coup that ousted Robert Mugabe in 2017, as well as the elections and deadly protests of 2018.



Ambition

He has also reported from a host of other African countries, including the DRC, Niger, Somalia, Zambia, Benin, Senegal, Tanzania and many more. His public and media relations experience includes serving briefly, as the spokesperson for the Gauteng MEC for Infrastructure Development, From late 2012 to mid-2013. This role involved communicating the official positions of the MEC who headed up one of the most challenging departments in the province. He frequently writes opinion pieces, which have been published in some of the country's leading newspapers. He is currently studying for an LLB and plans to practice as an advocate in the coming years.
